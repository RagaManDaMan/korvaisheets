Understanding Carnatic Korvais & Yatis

×

What is a Korvai?

A Korvai is a structured rhythmic composition in Carnatic music. It consists of two main parts: the Purvangam (initial structured section) and the Uttaranga (resolving sequence, often repeated 3 times as a tihai or arudi).

How to Read numbers in KorvaiSheets

A Korvai is defined as a series of numbers. Positive numbers denote phrase lengths, while gaps (karvais) are represented by intervals. The app alternates between phrases and gaps:

  • 6, 1, 6, 1, 6 means: phrase of 6, gap of 1, phrase of 6, gap of 1, phrase of 6. Total = 20 matras.
  • In classical notation, this displays as tt,ktt , tt,ktt , tt,ktt where , represents a 1-matra gap.

Yati Shapes

Yati refers to the structural envelope of a rhythmic phrase sequence:

  • Sama Yati: Constant width (e.g., 6-6-6)
  • Srotovaha Yati: Widening like a river (e.g., 5-6-7 or 3-5-7)
  • Gopuccha Yati: Tapering like a cow's tail (e.g., 7-5-3)
  • Mridangam Yati: Wide center, narrow ends (e.g., 3-7-3)
  • Damaruka Yati: Narrow center, wide ends (e.g., 7-3-7)

Math Alignment Challenge

A key skill for percussionists is adapting a korvai to fit the Tala cycle. For instance, in Adi Tala (32 matras in 1 cycle, 64 in 2 cycles), a korvai totalling 60 matras leaves a deficit of 4. We resolve this by adding 2 matras (karvais) to the end of the first two lines, making the final structure 22 + 22 + 20 = 64 matras!
